SECTION 1: ABOUT LUMIÈRE
================================================================

What is Lumière?
Lumière is a premium skincare brand founded in 2018, dedicated to crafting
science-backed, clean beauty products that deliver visible results. Every
formula is developed with leading dermatologists and tested on all skin types.
Our name is the French word for "light" — because we believe great skin is
radiant skin.

Who founded Lumière?
Lumière was founded by Dr. Isabelle Fontaine, a board-certified dermatologist
with 20 years of clinical experience, and her business partner Marc Devereaux,
a former cosmetic chemist at L'Oréal Research. Together they set out to bridge
the gap between pharmaceutical efficacy and clean beauty values.

What is Lumière's mission?
Our mission is simple: deliver dermatologist-grade skincare results using only
clean, ethically sourced ingredients — without compromise. We believe you should
never have to choose between what works and what is safe.

What makes Lumière different from other skincare brands?
We combine pharmaceutical-grade active ingredients with clean, plant-based
carriers. Our products are free from parabens, sulphates, synthetic fragrances,
mineral oils, and microplastics. Every product undergoes 18 months of clinical
testing before launch. We partner with independent dermatologists to validate
every claim we make.

Are Lumière products cruelty-free?
Yes. Lumière is certified cruelty-free by both Leaping Bunny and PETA's Beauty
Without Bunnies programme. We never test on animals at any stage of production,
and we do not sell in markets that require animal testing by law.

Are Lumière products vegan?
95% of our range is vegan. The exceptions are our Renewal Eye Cream and Royal
Honey Mask, which contain ethically sourced beeswax and honey from certified
sustainable apiaries in New Zealand. These products are clearly labelled on our
website with a non-vegan icon.

Are Lumière products suitable for sensitive skin?
Yes. All products are dermatologist-tested and formulated for sensitive skin
compatibility. We recommend patch testing any new product on your inner arm
for 24–48 hours before full facial use. If you have a known allergy, review the
full ingredient list on each product page before purchasing.

Are Lumière products safe during pregnancy?
We recommend consulting your doctor or dermatologist before using any skincare
during pregnancy. Products to avoid during pregnancy include our Vitamin C Glow
Serum (high-dose L-ascorbic acid), Retinol Night Cream (retinol), and AHA
Resurfacing Toner (glycolic acid). Our Hydra-Calm Cleanser, Moisture Surge
Moisturiser, and SPF 50 Glow Shield are generally considered safe, but please
always seek personalised medical advice.

Are Lumière products safe for breastfeeding?
Similar guidance applies to breastfeeding as pregnancy. Avoid retinol and
high-dose active serums. Our gentle hydrating and cleansing products are
typically considered safe. Consult your healthcare provider for personalised
advice.

Are Lumière products suitable for teenagers?
Yes, with guidance. We recommend our Hydra-Calm Cleanser, Moisture Surge
Moisturiser, and SPF 50 Glow Shield for teenagers. Active serums like Retinol
Night Cream and AHA Toner are best introduced from age 18 with gradual
introduction. Vitamin C serum is suitable from age 16.

Are Lumière products safe for mature skin (60+)?
Absolutely. Many of our products are specifically beneficial for mature skin,
especially our Retinol Night Cream, Renewal Eye Cream, and Moisture Surge
Moisturiser. The key is starting with lower frequencies and building up
tolerance gradually.

Where are Lumière products made?
All Lumière products are formulated and manufactured in our GMP-certified
(Good Manufacturing Practice) facility in Lausanne, Switzerland. Our packaging
is produced in Portugal using recycled and recyclable materials.

Is Lumière a dermatologist brand?
Yes. Our formulations are developed alongside a board of consulting
dermatologists. However, Lumière is a retail skincare brand — we do not
provide medical diagnoses or prescribe treatments. Always consult a licensed
dermatologist for skin conditions requiring medical treatment.

Does Lumière conduct clinical trials?
Yes. Every product undergoes a minimum 18-month clinical testing programme
before launch, including: RIPT (Repeat Insult Patch Test) for allergen safety,
SPF testing (for sun protection products), independent efficacy trials with
at least 50 participants, and dermatologist sign-off. Clinical data summaries
are available on each product page.

Has Lumière won any awards?
Yes. Lumière has been recognised with:
- Best Clean Skincare Brand — CEW Beauty Awards 2024
- Editor's Choice — Allure Magazine 2023 and 2024
- Best Serum — Harper's Bazaar Beauty Awards 2023 (Vitamin C Glow Serum)
- Sustainability Leader — Positive Luxury Awards 2024

VITAMIN C GLOW SERUM
====================

What does the Vitamin C Glow Serum do?
It brightens skin tone, fades dark spots and hyperpigmentation, neutralises
free radical damage from pollution and UV, and improves overall luminosity.
Results are typically visible within 4–8 weeks of consistent daily use.

What concentration of Vitamin C is in the serum?
15% L-ascorbic acid — the clinically proven, most bioavailable form of
Vitamin C. This is the upper effective range for home use without causing
irritation in most skin types.

Why does my Vitamin C Serum sting slightly on application?
A mild tingling is normal, especially on first use, due to the low pH required
for L-ascorbic acid to be effective. If the stinging is intense or prolonged
(more than 30 seconds), dilute with a drop of moisturiser or reduce use to
every other day. If stinging persists, discontinue and contact us.

My Vitamin C Serum has turned orange/brown. Is it still good?
No. Oxidation causes the serum to darken from pale yellow to orange or brown.
An oxidised serum has lost its efficacy and may cause breakouts or irritation.
You should replace it. To extend serum life: store in a cool, dark place,
ensure the lid is tightly closed after each use, and keep it away from
bathrooms (humidity accelerates oxidation).

How long does the Vitamin C Serum last once opened?
6 months from opening (PAO symbol on packaging). This is shorter than our other
products due to the inherent instability of L-ascorbic acid.

Can I use Vitamin C Serum around my eyes?
Apply up to, but not directly on, the eye area. For targeted eye treatment, use
our Renewal Eye Cream. Avoid getting serum directly in the eyes.

RETINOL NIGHT CREAM
===================

What does the Retinol Night Cream do?
It accelerates cell turnover, reduces the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles,
improves skin texture, fades dark spots, and stimulates collagen production.
Clinical studies show visible improvement in 12 weeks of consistent use.

What percentage of retinol is in the Night Cream?
0.5% encapsulated retinol. Encapsulation means the retinol is released slowly
over several hours, dramatically reducing irritation compared to unencapsulated
forms, while maintaining efficacy.

How should I introduce the Retinol Night Cream?
Week 1–2: Apply every third night.
Week 3–4: Apply every other night.
Week 5+: Apply every night if tolerated.
Always apply to dry skin (wait 10–15 minutes after cleansing to reduce
irritation) and follow with moisturiser.

Is skin purging normal with retinol?
Yes. Purging can occur in the first 2–6 weeks as cell turnover accelerates.
It may look like increased breakouts, mild flaking, or dryness. This is
temporary. If purging is severe or persists beyond 8 weeks, discontinue use
and contact us. To minimise: start slowly, use a gentle cleanser, and always
moisturise after application.

Can I use retinol in summer?
Yes, but with diligence. Retinol increases photosensitivity. Always wear SPF 30+
(we recommend SPF 50 Glow Shield) every morning when using retinol, year-round.
Apply retinol in the evening only — never in the morning.

Can I use retinol with Vitamin C?
Yes, but not at the same time. Apply Vitamin C Glow Serum in the morning and
Retinol Night Cream in the evening. Layering them together can destabilise both
actives and may cause irritation.

HYDRA-CALM CLEANSER
====================

What skin types is the Hydra-Calm Cleanser suitable for?
All skin types, with particular benefit for sensitive, dry, reactive, and
eczema-prone skin. It removes makeup and impurities without stripping the
skin's natural moisture barrier.

Does the Hydra-Calm Cleanser remove waterproof makeup?
It removes light to moderate makeup effectively. For heavy or waterproof
makeup, we recommend a pre-cleanse with a balm or micellar water, then
follow with the Hydra-Calm Cleanser as your second cleanse.

Should I use the cleanser morning and evening?
Yes. Cleansing morning and evening is recommended. In the morning, it removes
oils and overnight product residue. In the evening, it removes makeup,
sunscreen, and environmental pollutants.

MOISTURE SURGE MOISTURISER
===========================

How long does the 72-hour hydration last?
The 72-hour hydration claim refers to the moisturiser's ability to maintain
skin hydration levels for up to 72 hours in controlled clinical testing when
applied once. For daily skincare routines, we recommend applying morning and
evening for best results.

Is the Moisture Surge Moisturiser suitable for oily skin?
Yes. The formula is non-comedogenic and oil-free. The hyaluronic acid complex
provides hydration without adding oil, making it suitable for oily and
combination skin types. A lighter layer is sufficient for oily skin.

Can I use the Moisture Surge Moisturiser as my sole morning moisturiser under SPF?
Yes. Apply the moisturiser, let it absorb for 1–2 minutes, then apply SPF 50
Glow Shield on top.

Is the Moisture Surge Moisturiser available as a refill?
Yes. The Refill Pod is available at 25% off the full price. Return your empty
glass jar using our prepaid envelope and receive 50 Glow Rewards points.

SPF 50 GLOW SHIELD
==================

Is the SPF 50 Glow Shield chemical or mineral sunscreen?
It is a hybrid formula combining mineral filters (zinc oxide) with chemical
filters (tinosorb S and M) for broad-spectrum UVA/UVB protection. The result
is a lightweight, no white-cast formula suitable for all skin tones.

Can I reapply SPF 50 Glow Shield over makeup?
Yes. The lightweight gel-serum texture layers well over makeup. Press (do not
rub) gently over your face using clean fingertips for touch-up applications.

Does the SPF 50 Glow Shield leave a white cast?
No. The formula is specifically engineered to be invisible on all skin tones
including deeper skin tones. The zinc oxide particles are coated to prevent
the white cast typical of mineral sunscreens.

How often should I reapply sunscreen?
Every 2 hours of direct sun exposure, or immediately after swimming or heavy
sweating. One morning application provides protection for normal daily indoor
and outdoor activities with limited prolonged sun exposure.

Is SPF 50 Glow Shield water-resistant?
It provides 40 minutes of water resistance. If you are swimming or sweating
heavily, reapply after 40 minutes.

RENEWAL EYE CREAM
=================

What does the Renewal Eye Cream target?
Dark circles, puffiness, fine lines, and crow's feet. The peptide complex
(Matrixyl 3000 + Argireline) firms the delicate skin around the eye, while
caffeine and niacinamide reduce puffiness and discolouration.

How do I apply the Renewal Eye Cream?
Using your ring finger (lightest pressure), gently pat a small amount (rice
grain size per eye) along the orbital bone — the bony ridge around the eye.
Never pull or rub the eye area. Apply morning and evening.

Is the Renewal Eye Cream suitable for contact lens wearers?
Yes, but apply before inserting contact lenses and ensure no product gets
directly in the eye.

AHA RESURFACING TONER
======================

What does the AHA Resurfacing Toner do?
10% glycolic acid exfoliates the top layer of dead skin cells, revealing
smoother, brighter skin underneath. It also improves the absorption of serums
and moisturisers applied afterwards, and reduces the appearance of pores
and uneven texture over time.

How do I use the AHA Resurfacing Toner?
Apply to clean, dry skin using a cotton pad. Sweep gently over the face,
avoiding the eye area and lips. Follow immediately with serum and moisturiser.
Do not rinse off.

How often can I use the AHA Toner?
Start 2–3 times per week. Build up to daily use over 4–6 weeks as your skin
adapts. Always wear SPF during the day when using AHAs. Do not use the AHA
Toner on the same evening as retinol — alternate nights.

Is my skin peeling because of the AHA Toner?
Mild flaking is normal during the first few weeks of AHA use. Reduce frequency
to once or twice a week until your skin adjusts. If peeling is severe, red,
or accompanied by burning, discontinue use and contact us. Always moisturise
after using the toner.

BAKUCHIOL BALANCING SERUM
=========================

What is bakuchiol and why is it different from retinol?
Bakuchiol is a plant-derived compound from the Psoralea corylifolia plant.
It has been shown in clinical studies to produce similar anti-ageing results to
retinol (improved firmness, reduced fine lines, improved texture) without
causing irritation, photosensitivity, or purging. It is suitable for use
morning and evening, during pregnancy (with physician approval), and for
sensitive skin types that cannot tolerate retinol.

Can I use bakuchiol and retinol together?
Not necessary — they target similar pathways. Choose one or the other based
on your skin tolerance. If you're new to actives, start with bakuchiol.
If you are pregnant or breastfeeding, consult your doctor before use.

CERAMIDE BARRIER CREAM
======================

Who should use the Ceramide Barrier Cream?
Anyone with dry, compromised, flaky, or barrier-damaged skin. Particularly
recommended for: eczema-prone skin during flare-ups, post-procedure recovery
(after laser, chemical peels, or microneedling), winter skin repair, and as
an overnight occlusive treatment.

Can I use the Ceramide Barrier Cream over my other Lumière products?
Yes. It works well as the final step of your evening routine, sealing in all
previous products. Apply as the last step before sleep.

NIACINAMIDE CLARITY SERUM
=========================

What does the Niacinamide Clarity Serum do?
10% niacinamide minimises the appearance of pores, regulates sebum production,
evens skin tone, and strengthens the skin's barrier function. It is suitable
for morning and evening use and layers well with almost all other actives.

Can I use niacinamide with Vitamin C?
Yes. Earlier concerns about these two ingredients causing a reaction were based
on outdated research. Modern formulations of both are stable together. You can
layer our Vitamin C Glow Serum and Niacinamide Clarity Serum without concern.

Can I use niacinamide with retinol?
Yes. Niacinamide is one of the best partners for retinol — it helps reduce
retinol-related irritation and dryness. Apply niacinamide first, let absorb,
then apply retinol.

SECTION 3: SKINCARE ROUTINES & PRODUCT COMBINATIONS
================================================================

What is the recommended morning routine?
1. Hydra-Calm Cleanser
2. AHA Resurfacing Toner (optional, 2–3×/week to start)
3. Niacinamide Clarity Serum (optional)
4. Vitamin C Glow Serum
5. Renewal Eye Cream
6. Moisture Surge Moisturiser
7. SPF 50 Glow Shield (always last)

What is the recommended evening routine?
1. Hydra-Calm Cleanser
2. AHA Resurfacing Toner (alternate nights with retinol)
3. Bakuchiol or Niacinamide Serum (optional)
4. Retinol Night Cream
5. Renewal Eye Cream
6. Moisture Surge Moisturiser
7. Ceramide Barrier Cream (optional, as final occlusive step)

What is the recommended weekly routine?
Use the Royal Honey Mask once or twice a week after cleansing, before serum.
Leave on for 10–15 minutes, rinse thoroughly, then continue with your serum
and moisturiser.

What is the recommended beginner routine (first-time Lumière user)?
Start simple and build:
Morning: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Moisture Surge Moisturiser → SPF 50 Glow Shield.
Evening: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Moisture Surge Moisturiser.
After 2–4 weeks, introduce Vitamin C Serum in the morning. After another 4 weeks,
introduce Retinol Night Cream 2–3 evenings per week.

What routine do you recommend for oily/acne-prone skin?
Morning: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Niacinamide Clarity Serum → Vitamin C Glow Serum
→ Moisture Surge Moisturiser (light layer) → SPF 50 Glow Shield.
Evening: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → AHA Resurfacing Toner (3×/week) → Niacinamide
Clarity Serum → Retinol Night Cream (build gradually) → Moisture Surge Moisturiser.

What routine do you recommend for dry/dehydrated skin?
Morning: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Vitamin C Glow Serum → Renewal Eye Cream →
Moisture Surge Moisturiser → SPF 50 Glow Shield.
Evening: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Bakuchiol Balancing Serum → Renewal Eye Cream →
Moisture Surge Moisturiser → Ceramide Barrier Cream.

What routine do you recommend for sensitive/reactive skin?
Morning: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Moisture Surge Moisturiser → SPF 50 Glow Shield.
Evening: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Bakuchiol Balancing Serum → Moisture Surge
Moisturiser → Ceramide Barrier Cream (if needed).
Avoid AHA Toner and Retinol until sensitivity is under control.
Patch test every new product.

What routine do you recommend for anti-ageing?
Morning: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Vitamin C Glow Serum → Renewal Eye Cream →
Moisture Surge Moisturiser → SPF 50 Glow Shield.
Evening: Hydra-Calm Cleanser → Niacinamide Clarity Serum → Retinol Night Cream
→ Renewal Eye Cream → Moisture Surge Moisturiser → Ceramide Barrier Cream
(2–3 nights per week as occlusive step).
Weekly: Royal Honey Mask.

Can I use the Vitamin C Serum and Retinol Night Cream in the same routine?
Use them at different times of day — Vitamin C in the morning, Retinol in the
evening. Do not layer them together.

Can I use the AHA Toner and Retinol on the same night?
We do not recommend it. Both increase cell turnover and photosensitivity. Alternate
nights: AHA Toner on Monday/Wednesday/Friday, Retinol on Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday.

How many products can I safely layer?
There is no strict maximum, but we recommend introducing one new product at a
time (one product per 2–4 weeks) so you can identify any reaction. A complete
routine should have cleanser, serum, eye cream, moisturiser, and SPF — anything
beyond that is enhancement, not necessity.

What ingredients should I never combine?
- Retinol + AHA/BHA (same time of day) — use on alternate evenings
- Vitamin C + AHA (same morning) — not harmful but can cause irritation
- High-dose Vitamin C + Niacinamide (at very high doses, may cause flushing;
  at our concentrations, fine to use together)
- Benzoyl peroxide + Retinol — avoid same application
- Multiple high-potency actives in one application — can overwhelm the barrier

SECTION 4: INGREDIENTS & FORMULATION
================================================================

Are your products free from parabens?
Yes. All Lumière products are 100% paraben-free.

Are your products free from sulphates?
Yes. No sodium lauryl sulphate (SLS) or sodium laureth sulphate (SLES) in any
Lumière product.

Are your products free from synthetic fragrances?
Yes. We use no synthetic fragrances. Some products contain naturally-derived
botanical scents from ingredients like rosehip, bakuchiol, and centella asiatica.
Full ingredient lists are available on each product page.

Are your products free from mineral oil?
Yes. We use squalane (from sugarcane) and plant-derived emollients instead of
petroleum-derived mineral oils.

Are your products free from microplastics?
Yes. No microbeads or microplastic ingredients are used in any Lumière product.

Are your products free from formaldehyde-releasing preservatives?
Yes. We use a combination of phenoxyethanol, ethylhexylglycerin, and vitamin E
as our preservation system, which meets EU and UK cosmetics regulations.

Where can I find the full ingredient list for a product?
Every product page on lumiereskin.com includes the full INCI (International
Nomenclature Cosmetic Ingredients) list. You can also email us at
support@lumiereskin.com for a printed copy.

Are any Lumière products safe for nut allergies?
We do not use common tree nut oils (almond, walnut, hazelnut) in our core range.
However, some products contain macadamia nut oil or shea butter derivatives.
Review the full INCI list for each product and consult your allergist if you
have severe nut allergies.

Do you use alcohol in your products?
We use fatty alcohols (cetyl alcohol, cetearyl alcohol) which are emollients
and actually beneficial for skin barrier function. We do not use drying
alcohols (ethanol, denatured alcohol, isopropyl alcohol) as primary ingredients.
Trace amounts of ethanol may appear in some formulas as a solubiliser.

Are Lumière products pH-balanced?
Yes. Our products are formulated at a pH appropriate to their active ingredients
and skin compatibility. The AHA Toner is formulated at pH 3.5–4.0 for glycolic
acid efficacy. Other products are formulated at skin-compatible pH 4.5–6.5.

SECTION 5: STORAGE & SHELF LIFE
================================================================

How long do Lumière products last once opened?
- Vitamin C Glow Serum: 6 months (abbreviated due to L-ascorbic acid instability)
- All other products: 12 months
The PAO (Period After Opening) symbol on packaging shows an open jar with a
number (e.g., "12M" = 12 months after opening).

How long do unopened products last?
Unopened products have a shelf life of 24–36 months from manufacture date when
stored correctly. Batch code and expiry information is printed on the base of
each product.

How should I store my Lumière products?
Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat. The Vitamin C
Glow Serum should ideally be kept at 8–15°C (a cool cabinet, not direct
refrigerator cold). Avoid storing in humid bathrooms.

Can I refrigerate my Lumière products?
Yes. Refrigerating products (especially the Vitamin C Serum) can extend their
freshness and stability. Ensure products are in sealed containers and away from
food. Do not freeze.

My product has changed texture or smell. What should I do?
If a product has changed significantly in texture, colour, or smell within its
PAO period, stop using it and contact us at support@lumiereskin.com with your
batch code. We will investigate and replace the product if necessary.

SECTION 10: SKIN REACTIONS & CONCERNS
================================================================

I had a reaction to a Lumière product. What should I do?
Stop using the product immediately. Rinse the affected area with cool water.
If you experience severe swelling, difficulty breathing, hives spreading beyond
the application area, or any sign of anaphylaxis, seek emergency medical
attention immediately. For mild reactions (redness, itching, mild rash), contact
us at support@lumiereskin.com — we will issue a full refund and our team of
estheticians can advise on alternatives suitable for your skin type.

What do I do if I get a Lumière product in my eye?
Rinse your eye immediately with clean, lukewarm water for at least 15 minutes.
If irritation persists, seek medical attention. Contact us and we will note the
incident for product safety records.

Is it normal for the AHA Toner to tingle?
A mild tingle on application is normal and indicates the glycolic acid is
active. If the tingle becomes a burn, rinse off immediately with cool water,
skip the next application, and try every 3–4 days instead.

Is it normal for my skin to purge when I start using the Retinol Night Cream?
Skin purging can occur with retinol, especially in the first 2–6 weeks.
Purging involves increased breakouts as your skin accelerates cell turnover —
this brings congestion to the surface faster than normal. Purging is temporary.
If it persists beyond 8 weeks or is severe, discontinue use and contact us.

My skin is peeling after using the AHA Toner. Is this normal?
Mild flaking is normal in the adjustment period. Reduce use frequency to
1–2 times per week until your skin adapts. Always moisturise after the toner
and wear SPF daily. If peeling is severe or accompanied by redness and burning,
discontinue use.

Can I use Lumière products if I have eczema?
Many customers with eczema use our Hydra-Calm Cleanser and Ceramide Barrier
Cream successfully — both are formulated for compromised barrier function.
We recommend avoiding AHAs, retinol, and vitamin C during active eczema flares.
Consult your dermatologist before introducing new products during a flare.

Can I use Lumière products if I have rosacea?
Customers with rosacea often tolerate our Hydra-Calm Cleanser, Moisture Surge
Moisturiser, and SPF 50 Glow Shield well. Avoid AHA Toner, Vitamin C Serum, and
Retinol Night Cream during rosacea flares. The Bakuchiol Balancing Serum and
Niacinamide Clarity Serum may help calm redness. Always consult your dermatologist.

Can I use Lumière products if I have psoriasis?
Psoriasis is a medical condition managed in collaboration with dermatologists.
Our Ceramide Barrier Cream may support barrier repair between flares, but we
cannot make medical claims about psoriasis treatment. Please consult your doctor.

Can I use Lumière products if I have acne?
Yes. The Niacinamide Clarity Serum, AHA Resurfacing Toner, and Retinol Night
Cream are all helpful for acne-prone skin. Start slowly and introduce one active
at a time. Our Hydra-Calm Cleanser is gentle enough for daily use on acne-prone
skin. Avoid heavy occlusive products during active breakouts.

Can I use Lumière products if I have melasma?
Our Vitamin C Glow Serum and AHA Resurfacing Toner can help manage melasma,
but SPF is the most critical part of any melasma routine — use SPF 50 Glow
Shield every single day. Melasma is a complex condition; we recommend working
with a dermatologist for a comprehensive treatment plan.

Can I use Lumière products after a chemical peel or laser treatment?
Always follow your dermatologist's post-procedure instructions first. As a
general guide: use only the Hydra-Calm Cleanser and Ceramide Barrier Cream for
the first 7–14 days after a procedure. Reintroduce active serums only after
your skin has fully healed and with dermatologist clearance.
